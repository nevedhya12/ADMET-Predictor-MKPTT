- Before I forget which descriptors I have added and why them? below is the full list of what and why:
	- MW (molecular weight): Absorption (Lipinski cutoff ~500), renal clearance favors smaller molecules.
	- LogP		       : Membrane permeability, CYP metabolism likelihood, toxicity via promiscuous binding.
	- TPSA		       : Strong predictor of oral absorption and blood-brain barrier crossing.
	- HBD (H-bond donors)  : Absorption, Lipinski rule of 5.
	- HBA (H-bond acceptor): Absorption, Lipinski rule of 5.
	- RotBonds	       : Oral bioavailability, binding/clearance behavior.
	- AromaticRings        : Metabolic stability, CYP liability, toxicity risk.
	- FormalCharge         : Distribution, renal excretion (charged species clear differently).
	- MolarRefractivity    : Correlates with distribution and metabolism behavior.
	- Fsp3		       : Metabolic stability; flat molecules are linked to promiscuity/toxicity.
	- QED                  : Single summary score of how "normal" a molecule looks overall.
	- AmideBondCount       : Directly flags peptide-like structures — this is your "crazy structure" signal.
	- MaxRingSize          : Macrocycles (rings >12 atoms) are structurally unlike typical drug-like rings.
	- BertzCT              : Single number that naturally separates simple molecules from tangled/unusual ones.
	- Stereocenter         : Peptides and complex natural products tend to have many; simple molecules have few.
